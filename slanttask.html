Scrolling in web design
The long and short of it
Long Scroll:
THE GOOD STUFF

There's more visual freedom in parallel website design, Rather then
focusing an block style information architecture, you can create
refreshing and engaging visuals such as animations or beautiful
photography, that act as a continum, ultimately inspring users to
scroll futher down the page.

STORY Telling OPPURTUNITY

Do you want to tell a story eith your product or service?
long scrolling lends itself well to a narrative and
encourages meaningful interation from users.

SIMPLE INTERFACE FOR SMART DEVICES USERS

Mobile and tablets have evolved the way we engage in websites. It's
now less about clicking and more about scrolling, swiping and tapping,
Long-scrolling websites take away the barrier of complex navigation,
providing a responsive,userfriendly experience for mobile users.

STRAIGHT TO THE POINT MESSAGING

Parallax website design is a great solution for us time-poor
website browsers, as it forces businesses to conver their
message and vision into one dynamic web design page.

FASTER FOR USERS TO REALIZE THEIR GOAL

These days, Scrolling is so simple that iy's become an
instinctive action, whereas clicking through to another page
requries through and motivation. A single page website
allows user to identifies their purposeon the website quicker
that needing to still through multiple pages.

LONG SCROLL:THE NOT SO GOOD STUFF
YOU NEED TO BE SEARCH ENGINE SALVY

One of the biggest problems that parallax websites face is that
they are not SEO friendly.Why?When you limit the number of
pages on your website,Google has lowe pages to index and
negatively impacts your standing on the search engine results
page(SFRP).Luckily there are ways around this.

SLOWER LOAD TIMES

50% of wesites users bounce of the page if it hasn't loaded
in 3.5 seconds. So the more high resolution imagery and
animation you include, the longest it takes to load the page.
When writing this article, we listed some popular parallax
websites, and found that the ones with complex visuals took
up to 30 seconds to load on a mobile device, whereas simple
and minimal long scroll websites loaded with a few seconds.

AWKWARD NAVIGATION

Instead of hitting the "back" button, users must scroll back
up the page which can be frustrating for users who want to
refer back to a certain element. An easy way around this is
to include top navigation and have each element anchored
to a category or section.
